# Brand Identity: The Insightful Explorer

70% Sage + 30% Adventurer Archetype Color Palette with Hex Codes and Usage Percentages

## Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage % | Role |
|-------|-----|---------|------|
| Sage Green | #98A869 | 25% | Primary background, main brand identity |
| Muted Teal Green | #738276 | 20% | Secondary backgrounds, content areas, section dividers |
| Warm Beige | #B2AC88 | 15% | Neutral backgrounds, body text backgrounds, soft transitions |
| Charcoal Gray | #4D5D53 | 10% | Primary text color, borders, subtle accents |
| Olive Green | #6A760C | 15% | Action elements, interactive components, emphasis |
| Burnt Orange | #D9741A | 10% | Call-to-action buttons, important alerts, energy accents |
| Deep Blue | #34788C | 5% | Links, small accents, trust elements |

## Primary Colors (70% - Sage Archetype)

- **Sage Green #98A869 (25%)**: Website backgrounds, business cards, primary branding elements
- **Muted Teal Green #738276 (20%)**: Sidebar backgrounds, card backgrounds, secondary brand elements
- **Warm Beige #B2AC88 (15%)**: Page backgrounds, text containers, subtle overlays
- **Charcoal Gray #4D5D53 (10%)**: Headings, body text, form borders, icons

## Secondary Colors (30% - Adventurer Archetype)

- **Olive Green #6A760C (15%)**: Navigation highlights, hover states, feature callouts
- **Burnt Orange #D9741A (10%)**: Primary buttons, notifications, promotional elements
- **Deep Blue #34788C (5%)**: Hyperlinks, badges, secondary buttons, icons

## Implementation Guidelines

- **70% Sage Foundation**: Use the four sage colors as your base palette, creating a calm, wise, and trustworthy foundation that dominates your visual identity.
- **30% Adventurer Energy**: Strategically incorporate the three adventurer colors as accent elements to add energy, action, and exploration spirit without overwhelming the sage foundation.
- **Contrast Ratios**: Ensure accessibility by using the charcoal gray (#4D5D53) for text over light backgrounds, and light colors over the deeper olive green and burnt orange backgrounds.
- **Visual Hierarchy**: Use the burnt orange (#D9741A) sparingly for highest priority elements, olive green (#6A760C) for secondary actions, and deep blue (#34788C) for informational elements.
